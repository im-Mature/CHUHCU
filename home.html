THE MARCOS REGIME

Known for his statement, “I shall declare Martial Law”, Ferdinand Marcos was head of state in the Philippines from 1966 to 1986. His dictatorship was put into an end by an uprising of people in EDSA, or Epifanio de los Santos Avenue. Before he became the president of the Philippines, he was a lawyer, a member of the Philippine House representatives (1949-1959), and a member of the Philippine Senate (1959-1965).

Being the president of the Philippines, Marcos made ambitious public works to improve the life of the people in the Philippines. One of these, was a land reform program that purposed to alienate the powerful landowner elites. Another achievement was to be a part of, or rather, to have Philippines as one of the founding members of ASEAN or the Association of Southeast Asian Nations. Due to his good impacts during his presidency, he was elected again, making him the 1st president to be reelected. But with his second rule, things were beginning to fall. The economic growth slowed, crime rates had increased and uprising groups were formed like, The Communist Party of the Philippines and the New People’s Army.

Due to the fact that communism has increasing threats, Marcos declared Proclamation 1081 putting the Philippines under Martial Law. He signed the law on Sept.21, 1972 and announced it in television on Sept.23, 1972 at 7:15 pm. Orders were made to support martial law, like, putting all powers to the hand of the president, arresting people who wants to take over the government, curfews and banning of groups. But after a few years, Martial Law was put into an end on January 17, 1981.
